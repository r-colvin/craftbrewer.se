### 🤖 Assistant

Nice setup 🍻 A keg system is pretty low-maintenance once you get a rhythm. Here’s the homebrew-tested, real-world guidance most folks follow.

---

## 1. How often should you **clean the beer lines**?
**Every 2–4 weeks** is the sweet spot.

- **Commercial breweries:** every 2 weeks  
- **Home kegerators:**  
  - Every **2 weeks** if you drink a lot or pour daily  
  - Every **4 weeks** if usage is light  

**Also clean immediately if:**
- You change beer styles (especially dark → light, sour → clean)
- You notice off-flavors, haze, or slow pours

👉 Use a proper **beer line cleaner (BLC)**, not just sanitizer.

---

## 2. How often should you **disassemble and clean the taps**?
**Every 1–2 months**, or sooner if needed.

- If you pour frequently → **monthly**
- Light use → **every 2 months**
- Always disassemble if:
  - The tap sticks
  - Beer dries in the faucet
  - Fruit/sugary beers were on tap

🚨 Forward-sealing taps (Perlick-style) can stretch this a bit, but they’re not maintenance-free.

---

## 3. How often should you **sanitize beer lines and taps**?
Sanitizing ≠ cleaning — do it **after cleaning**, not instead of.

**Best practice:**
- **Clean with BLC → rinse → sanitize**
- Sanitize:
  - After every cleaning cycle
  - Before tapping a new keg
  - After reassembling taps

Star San is perfect here. No need to rinse after sanitizing.

---

## 4. What to do if you’re going away and turning the fridge off
This is important — dried beer = microbial nightmare.

### If you’ll be gone **more than a few days**:
**Do this before you leave:**

1. **Remove the keg(s)**
2. **Clean the lines thoroughly** with BLC
3. **Rinse well**
4. **Push sanitizer through the lines**
5. **Leave sanitizer sitting in the lines and taps**
6. Optionally:
   - Disassemble taps and let them air-dry
   - OR leave them assembled but wet with sanitizer

💡 Leaving sanitizer in the system prevents mold, bacteria, and dried beer stone.

### When you return:
- Flush sanitizer with water or beer
- Pour and dump the first pint or two
- You’re good to go

---

## Quick cheat sheet
- **Line cleaning:** every **2–4 weeks**
- **Tap disassembly:** every **1–2 months**
- **Sanitize:** after every cleaning + before new keg
- **Going away:** clean → sanitize → leave sanitizer in lines

If they’re poured **at least once every 5–7 days**:

If a tap sits **&gt;14 days** with beer in it:
- Risk goes up:
  - Sticky tap
  - Dried beer stone
  - Wild yeast/lacto growth in the faucet
- At that point I’d want:
  - A purge pour **or**
  - A faucet rinse routine

Warm taps + residual beer =  
- faster microbial growth  
- faster drying of sugars in the faucet body  
- sticky seals and beer stone

## What I’d do *every time* you go away (fridge OFF)

Since you don’t want sanitizer sitting in the system, this is the **cleanest, lowest-risk routine**:

### Before you leave (10–15 minutes total)
1. **Disconnect liquid ball locks** from all beer/cider/wine kegs  
2. **Use your water/soda keg to flush each beer line + tap**
   - Open the DuoTight flow control
   - Push until beer is completely gone
3. **Keep flushing with cold water**
   - You’re just displacing beer, not sanitizing
4. **Shut flow control**
5. Optionally:
   - Crack the tap open briefly to drain
   - Or remove the spout if you want them bone-dry

Result:
- No sugar
- No sanitizer
- No pressure
- Nothing nasty drying inside
