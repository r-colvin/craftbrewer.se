---

### **1. Why do the instructions not mention sanitation?**

For your listed beverages:

- **Soda water (plain tap water)**
- **Tonic water from concentrate**
- **Julmust from Robertson’s concentrate**
- **KegLand Ready-to-Keg kits like Impressence Tonic**

The instructions often do **not mention sanitation** because:

1. **Low risk of fermentation**:  
   - These products are either **non-fermentable** or include additives that inhibit microbial growth (like FermStop in the Impressence Tonic).  
   - Tonic and Julmust concentrates often have **high sugar or salt content**, which limits bacterial growth before consumption.  
   - They are intended to be consumed quickly, so long-term microbial spoilage is less of a concern.

2. **Short shelf-life expectations**:  
   - Unlike beer, which might sit in a keg for weeks or months, these drinks are generally meant to be **consumed in days to a couple of weeks**. The shorter the time, the less chance for infection to develop.

3. **Not brewed products**:  
   - Beer involves **live yeast and fermentable sugars**, which are highly susceptible to wild contamination.  
   - Ready-to-use concentrates have already been **pasteurized or chemically stabilized**.  

So, the **risk of “infection” is lower**, and that’s why instructions don’t emphasize sanitizing.

---

### **2. Does this mean cleaning the keg is enough?**

**Mostly, yes—but with nuance:**

- **Cleaning** removes visible residue and old flavors.  
- **Sanitizing** kills any microbes that could grow if they find sugars or other nutrients.  

Even if infection is low-risk:  

- **If your keg is clean, you’re probably safe**, especially for beverages meant to be consumed in a few days.  
- **For longer storage or more sensitive concentrates (like Julmust), sanitizing is still a good idea**.  
- **Your PET kegs and tapping equipment** should be rinsed thoroughly between uses to avoid off-flavors.

✅ **Rule of thumb:**  
- Short-term, low-sugar beverages → cleaning is usually sufficient.  
- Anything with sugar or left for &gt;1 week → sanitize.

---

### **3. Other considerations**

#### **Temperature**
- Keep **all these drinks cold (refrigerated)** once in the keg.  
  - Cold storage **slows microbial growth**.  
  - Room temperature storage increases the risk of spoilage, especially for Julmust.

#### **Carbonation**
- You have CO₂ cartridges and regulators. A few tips:
  - Carbonate to taste, but avoid overpressure in PET kegs.  
  - Soda water and tonic benefit from higher carbonation (~2.5–3 volumes CO₂).  
  - Julmust varies; follow concentrate instructions.

#### **Consumption window**
- **Soda water:** consume within a week if refrigerated.  
- **Tonic/Impressence:** consume within 1–2 weeks. FermStop helps here.  
- **Julmust:** concentrate may last a few weeks refrigerated; flavor may degrade after that.  

#### **Cross-contamination**
- **Avoid using the same keg for different flavors without thorough cleaning**. Residual flavors can carry over.  
- PET kegs absorb flavors more than stainless steel, so a good rinse is critical.

#### **FermStop**
- Only needed if you want to **prevent accidental fermentation**. For high-sugar concentrates, it adds extra safety.

| Other concentrate kits | Follow instructions; sanitize if sugary | Refrigerated | 1–2 weeks |

**Key points:**

- Cleaning is critical; sanitizing is optional for very short-term, low-sugar drinks.  
- Refrigerate everything to prolong freshness and prevent microbial growth.  
- Avoid flavor cross-contamination.  
- Watch carbonation levels, especially in PET kegs.
