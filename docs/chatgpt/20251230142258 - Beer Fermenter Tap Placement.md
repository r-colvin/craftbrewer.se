When creating your own beer fermenter from a PolyPropylene Food-Grade Plastic bucket (specifically https://www.witre.se/sv/wsw/livsmedelsgodkand-plasthink-med-lock-1794m415 - 20L, bottom diameter 281.6mm, height 327mm) with a KegLand HDPE tap (https://kegland.com.au/products/fermenter-tap-adjustable-spout-with-bulkhead-24mm-hole) how far from the bottom should I drill the hole to install the tap? 

Too low and it will overhang the bottom of the bucket, and may also be in the trub that will settle during fermentation/dry hopping; Too high and it will waste beer that sits below the tap!

My batch size will be around 10L, though may be more

## 1. Physical constraints of the bucket + tap

### Bucket
- **Bottom diameter:** 281.6 mm  
- **Height:** 327 mm  
- Like almost all plastic buckets, the base is **not flat**:
  - There is a **rounded transition (radius)** from the side wall to the bottom
  - The bucket usually sits on a **raised outer ring** for strength

In practice, this curved + raised base occupies **~15–20 mm of vertical height** before the wall becomes truly vertical.

### KegLand HDPE tap
- **Hole size:** 24 mm
- **Bulkhead + nut thickness:** ~8–10 mm inside the bucket
- **Tap body length below hole center:** ~20–25 mm

If you drill too low:
- The nut will sit on the curved base → leaks
- The tap will physically clash with the table/floor
- You’ll pull mostly trub

---

## 2. Trub and dry-hop considerations

Even with careful brewing, you should expect:
- **Hot + cold break + yeast cake:** ~20–30 mm depth
- **Dry hops (if used):** can add another 5–15 mm

So you want the tap **above ~30–40 mm of settled material**.

---

## 3. Beer loss vs practicality (10 L batch in a 20 L bucket)

Let’s quantify the “wasted” beer:

- Bucket radius ≈ 141 mm
- Cross-sectional area ≈ 0.062 m²
- **10 mm of height ≈ 0.62 L**

So if the tap is:
- **40 mm from bottom** → ~2.5 L below tap if completely full  
But in reality:
- That volume is **mostly trub + yeast**
- Clear beer loss is closer to **0.5–1 L**, which is normal for bucket fermenters

Trying to save that last half-liter usually causes **oxidation, clogs, or cloudy beer**.

---

## 4. Recommended drilling height (best compromise)

### ✅ **Drill the hole so the CENTER is 35–40 mm from the bottom**

**Why this works:**
- Clears the curved base and raised foot
- Leaves space for the bulkhead nut to seal properly
- Keeps the tap above the yeast/trub layer
- Avoids floor/table interference
- Industry-standard height for bucket fermenters

If you want a single number:

&gt; **👉 38 mm from the bottom to the center of the hole**

This is a very safe, proven placement.

---

## 5. Optional refinements

If you want to get fancy:
- Angle the tap **slightly upward inside** (many adjustable spouts allow this)
- Cold-crash before packaging to compact trub
- Use a short pickup tube or rotate the tap as the level drops
