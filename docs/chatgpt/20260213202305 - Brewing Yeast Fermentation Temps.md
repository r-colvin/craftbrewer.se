## Big picture first: wort temp ≠ room temp
During active fermentation, yeast metabolism generates heat. As a rule of thumb:

- **Active ale fermentations run ~2–5 °C (4–9 °F) warmer than ambient**
- **Lagers run ~1–3 °C (2–5 °F) warmer**, but for longer

So if you want a beer to ferment at **20 °C**, the room usually needs to be **16–18 °C**, depending on vessel size and activity.

## 🍺 What 23 °C means for fermentation

Yeast activity often runs **2–5 °C warmer than room temperature** during active fermentation. So:

- Room 23 °C → beer will ferment ~25–28 °C (depending on yeast activity and vessel)
- That’s on the **high end for many ale yeasts**
- At those temps, yeast tend to produce **strong esters and sometimes fusel alcohols** — which can be too fruity, solventy, or harsh

For a NEIPA you *do* want fruity esters — but ideally *soft* and juicy, not hot or solventy.

## 🍶 Practical tips for 23 °C fermentations

**If using the included LalBrew New England:**
- Try to keep beer temp as *low as possible* with airflow
  - Fan or gentle ice pack under fermenter can bring fermentation temp down 1–2 °C
- Avoid prolonged exposure &gt;26 °C — that’s where harsher esters/fusel can show
- Give extra time for yeast to fully attenuate — NEIPA strains under stress/speed can stall or produce off flavors

**If switching to Verdant IPA:**

- Flavor tends to be *juicy and bright even at slightly warmer temps*

**If switching to a cleaner ale yeast (e.g., US-05)**
- You’ll get a *cleaner backbone* but *less juicy character*
- Might serve you better if hop flavors feel muddled at 25–26 °C

## 2️⃣ Fermentation &amp; dry-hop timeline (Verdant, fridge-controlled)

### Day 0 – Pitch
- Chill wort to **18 °C**
- Oxygenate normally (shake or splash)
- Pitch **2 × 11 g LalBrew Verdant IPA**

---

### Days 1–3 – Primary fermentation (high kräusen)
- Leave fridge at **16 °C**
- Use **blow-off tube**
- Expect very active fermentation

Do **not** open the fermenter.

---

### Day 3 or 4 – First dry hop (biotransformation)
Criteria:
- Gravity ~50–60% attenuated  
- Kräusen starting to fall but still active

**Action**
- Add **100 g loose pellets**
- Gently open lid just enough to dump hops
- Close immediately

Keep fridge at **16 °C**

---

### Day 6 or 7 – Second dry hop
- Fermentation mostly done
- Gravity near final

**Action**
- Add remaining **100 g loose pellets**
- Same quick open-and-close approach

At this point:
- You can raise fridge to **18–19 °C** if you want to ensure full attenuation
- Hold for **2–3 days**

---

### Day 9–10 – Diacetyl cleanup complete
- Gravity stable for 2 days
- No butter / slickness

---

## 3️⃣ Cold crash (very helpful for losses &amp; clarity)

### Day 10 → 12
- Drop fridge to **2–4 °C**
- Hold **48 hours**

Effects:
- Hop particles compact hard
- Yeast drops out
- Much cleaner transfer
- Less hop burn

⚠️ Keep airlock/blow-off protected (sanitized liquid or CO₂ balloon if possible).

## 4️⃣ Closed transfer to Oxebar PET kegs

### Before transfer
- Clean and sanitize kegs
- **CO₂ flush each keg** (very important for NEIPA)
- Chill kegs if possible

### Transfer method (bucket)
- Gravity transfer with hose to bottom of keg
- Keep hose submerged
- Fill slowly
- Stop transfer as soon as beer turns hazy/green

Expect to intentionally leave **~1 L** behind.

## 5️⃣ Carbonation plan (slow, serving-pressure method)

### Target carbonation for NEIPA
- **~2.2–2.4 volumes CO₂**
- Soft, creamy mouthfeel (fits style)

### Regulator setup
- DuoTight mini regulator
- Set to **10–12 PSI (0.7–0.8 bar)**
- Beer temp: ideally **2–4 °C**

### Carbonation method
- Connect gas
- Leave at serving pressure
- **5–7 days** to fully carbonate
- Sample after day 4

This method is:
- Gentle on hop aroma
- Uses less CO₂
- Very consistent

## 6️⃣ How many 16 g CO₂ bulbs?

### Carbonation CO₂ needs (rule-of-thumb)
- Carbonating beer uses roughly **4–5 g CO₂ per liter** (depends on temp &amp; target volumes)

For **~10–11 L total beer**:
- CO₂ dissolved into beer: ~45–55 g

Each bulb = **16 g**
- Ideal gas only → ~3.5 bulbs
- Real-world inefficiency → **4–5 bulbs**

### Serving CO₂
- Small pours, short lines, PET kegs
- Expect **~1 bulb per keg** for serving

### ✅ Realistic total
- **Carbonation:** 4–5 bulbs
- **Serving:** 2–3 bulbs
- **Total:** **~6–8 × 16 g bulbs**

(Always keep one spare — pressure creep happens.)

This option makes more sense for **kveik**, not Verdant.

➡️ **Do NOT free-rise with Verdant in your setup.**
