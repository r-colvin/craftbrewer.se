# Toxicology and migration

:::note Status

:::

**Covers:** Ranked risk framework for degradation products and migrating substances from homebrewing materials. What is actually dangerous, what is theoretical, and what is negligible. The "sloppy brewer" scenario — what happens to someone who never cleans properly.

**Risks to rank (highest to lowest):**
1. NR/SBR elastomers — N-nitrosamine precursors. Invisible to inspection. Only safeguard is material selection. Non-negotiable replacement recommendation.
2. POM (acetal) — formaldehyde migration under degradation. Historical material, largely replaced, but still present in some John Guest fittings.
3. PC (polycarbonate) — BPA migration. Largely eliminated from current equipment but still found in older gear.
4. PS (polystyrene) — styrene migration. Airlock context. GPPS vs. SAN distinction.
5. DDBSA in beer from ABNS residue — worst case 0.21 ppm, far below flavour or safety thresholds.
6. Microplastics — sources predominantly environmental; glass bottles measured higher than PET in ANSES 2025 data.

**Key principle:** Visible failure before toxicology — in most cases, material degradation is visible (cracking, swelling, discolouration) before toxic migration becomes significant. NR/SBR is the critical exception: nitrosamine precursor release is chemically invisible.

**Regulatory framework:** EU Regulation 1935/2004 (food contact general), EU Regulation 10/2011 (plastics specific), IARC classifications where relevant.
