# The wet-dry cycle model

:::note Status
**Stub** — content to be written.
:::

**Covers:** The fundamental failure mechanism in homebrew sanitation — liquid applied to a surface, water evaporates, non-volatile components concentrate to potentially damaging levels. The quantitative model: film residue after gravity drain, concentration factor (CF) derivation, CF=250 at complete drying, Zone A/B/C geometry, the cleaning reset mechanism.

**Key calculations to present:**
- Retained film on a 20–25 L fermenter after gravity drain: ~3.4 mL
- DDBSA at working dilution: ~300 ppm → at CF=250: ~75,000 ppm (7.5%)
- Drying time at 20°C, 50% RH, still air: ~8–12 hours to complete drying
- DDBSA in beer worst case: ~0.21 ppm — well below 1–5 ppm flavour threshold
- The cleaning reset: post-brew PBW removes accumulated non-volatile residue

**Zone definitions:**
- Zone A: open bore, smooth surface — WDC at face value
- Zone B: thread roots, confined geometry — WDC multiplied
- Zone C: compressed elastomer seal — absorbed, not evaporated
