# The documentation problem

:::note Status
**Stub** — content to be written.
:::

**Covers:** What vendors should provide for food-contact homebrewing equipment, what they actually provide, and why the gap matters. Not an attack on vendors — a statement of what the standard should be, illustrated with real examples from this research.

**The standard that should apply:** Every food-contact item should state, at minimum: the material of every food-contact component; the applicable food contact standard; any use limitations. In the product specification, not in a Q&A thread.

**Case studies from research:**
- ChemiPro San rinse/no-rinse: same manufacturer contradicts itself across product page, blog post, and verbal response. Swedish and English instructions differ. No regulatory approval for no-rinse use confirmed.
- Mr-Malt tap material: information exists only in a Q&A response, not traceable to a primary source.
- StellarSan SDS: Australian NOHSC format, not EU REACH — sold into EU market.
- KegLand DuoTight revision: POM collar replaced with POK, but POM body unaddressed, John Guest fittings unaddressed, no quantitative data provided.
- KegLand StellarSan storage guidance: contradicted between KegLand documentation and a US retailer. KegLand confirmed leave-in is correct; retailer's "one hour" caution was wrong but widely repeated.

**Regulatory framework:** EU Regulation 1935/2004; EU Regulation 528/2012 (biocides); what "food safe" claims do and do not guarantee.

**Tone note:** Measured, factual, not confrontational. Absence of documentation is not evidence of danger — it is evidence of an information gap. The reader deserves to know when they are working with incomplete information.
