# Bucket fermenters

:::note Status

:::

**Covers:** The standard plastic bucket fermenter in its three common configurations for the Swedish market. Per-component material analysis, compatibility ratings, and practical recommendations.

**Configurations:**
1. **Bryggbolaget / generic** — PP bucket, unknown tap material (likely PP or HDPE), unknown grommet material (often NR — replace immediately)
2. **Witre + KegLand** — Witre PP bucket (EU 1935/2004 and 10/2011 compliant, migration tested by J.S. Hamilton Poland), KegLand HDPE tap, EPDM grommet (assumed — black colour consistent; confirmation pending from KegLand EU)
3. **Generic bucket, upgraded components** — reader-assembled from available parts

**Per-component analysis:**
- Bucket body (PP): A-rated for ABNS and DES; EU food contact compliance documentation available for Witre/Plast-Box
- Tap (HDPE preferred over PP): HDPE rated slightly better for prolonged chemical contact; functional difference minimal at normal use
- Tap thread root — Zone B geometry: confined space, WDC multiplier applies; primary risk point; addressed by post-brew disassembly and cleaning
- Tap washer/gasket (silicone): B-rated for DDBSA — adequate; absorbs organics over time (service life concern, not safety concern)
- Grommet (EPDM vs. NR): NR rates D for DDBSA, carries N-nitrosamine precursor risk, invisible to inspection — replace with EPDM. Non-negotiable recommendation.
- Airlock (PP): A-rated
- Airlock fill liquid: StarSan solution or DES; not plain water (contamination risk); not vodka (unnecessary)

**Cleaning sequence:** Post-brew disassembly (tap must be removed); PBW soak; brush thread roots; inspect washer; rinse; dry; reassemble for storage.

**Cross-references:** Materials register (PP, HDPE, EPDM, NR, silicone ratings), WDC model (Zone B), Toxicology (NR nitrosamines).
