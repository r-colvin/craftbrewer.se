# Bottles and bottling

:::note Status
**Stub** — content to be written.
:::

**Covers:** PET bottles, glass bottles, crown caps, bottling wands, and the complete gravity bottling process with oxygen management.

**PET bottles:**
- Food contact compliant; reusable; recyclable
- OTR (oxygen transmission rate) higher than multilayer Oxebar — relevant for hop-forward styles and extended conditioning
- Squeeze-before-capping: removing headspace oxygen by squeezing the bottle before applying the cap — effective, simple, no equipment required
- Microplastics: ANSES 2025 data shows glass bottles measured higher than PET in some measurements — context explained

**Glass bottles:**
- Preferred for extended conditioning and oxygen-sensitive styles
- Heavier; breakage risk; harder to sanitise consistently
- Crown cap liner: oxygen-scavenging liners (e.g. Amorim Oxyguard) — do not sanitise with chemical sanitiser before use; the scavenging chemistry is deactivated by contact with sanitiser

**Bottling wand:**
- Bottom-fill method: minimises turbulence and oxygen pickup vs. top-fill
- Clean bore — inspect for residue at junction with tubing
- Silicone tubing: sanitise by boiling, ABNS flow-through, or DES flush

**Carbonation drops:**
- Consistent dosing; simple; no measuring required
- Solid drops into dry bottle before filling: less oxygen displacement than liquid priming solution in a separate vessel

**Oxygen management in gravity bottling:**
- Bottom-fill wand: primary mitigation
- Carbonation drops direct to bottle: secondary mitigation
- Squeeze-before-capping (PET): tertiary mitigation
- Honest limitation: headspace oxygen pickup is unavoidable without counter-pressure filling
