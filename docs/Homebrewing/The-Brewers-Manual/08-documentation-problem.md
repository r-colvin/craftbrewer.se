# The documentation problem

:::note Status
**Stub** — content to be written.
:::

**Covers:** What vendors should provide for food-contact homebrewing equipment, what they actually provide, and why the gap matters. Not an attack on vendors — a statement of what the standard should be, illustrated with real examples from this research.

**The standard that should apply:** Every food-contact item should state, at minimum: the material of every food-contact component; the applicable food contact standard; any use limitations. This information belongs in the product specification, not in a Q&A thread.

**Case studies from research:**
- ChemiPro San rinse/no-rinse: the same manufacturer contradicts itself across its product page, blog post, and verbal response. Swedish and English instructions differ. No regulatory approval for no-rinse use has been confirmed. Position: treat as rinse-required pending formal EU-compliant no-rinse approval.
- Mr-Malt tap material: information exists only in a Q&A response, not traceable to a primary source.
- KegLand StellarSan SDS: Australian NOHSC format, not EU REACH/CLP — sold into the EU market without an EU-compliant SDS.
- KegLand DuoTight revision: POM collar replaced with POK — the correct fix — but the fitting body material is not addressed, John Guest fittings are not addressed, and no quantitative data is provided to support the compatibility claim. See [POM](./05-materials-register/08-pom.md) and [POK](./05-materials-register/07-pok.md).
- KegLand StellarSan storage guidance: contradicted between KegLand's own documentation and a widely cited US retailer. KegLand confirmed leave-in storage is safe; the US retailer's "one hour maximum" caution was incorrect but widely repeated. See [PET](./05-materials-register/03-pet.md).

**Regulatory framework:** [EU Regulation 1935/2004](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02004R1935-20090807); [EU Regulation 528/2012](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02012R0528-20231101) (biocides); what "food safe" claims do and do not guarantee.

**Tone:** Measured, factual, not confrontational. Absence of documentation is not evidence of danger — it is evidence of an information gap. The reader deserves to know when they are working with incomplete information.
