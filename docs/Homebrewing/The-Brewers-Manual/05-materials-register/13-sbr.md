# Styrene-butadiene rubber (SBR)

| | ABNS | DES | PBW | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | *D* | *C–D* | — | *D* |

:::danger Replace immediately
SBR shares every concern noted for natural rubber (NR). Replace with EPDM immediately.
:::

SBR is a synthetic rubber used as an inexpensive alternative to NR. Like NR, it has an unsaturated backbone (from the butadiene component), making it susceptible to oxidative degradation and chemical attack by ABNS. Like NR, it is typically sulphur-cured with accelerators — thiuram and dithiocarbamate compounds — that produce N-nitrosamine precursors in contact media.

SBR has no advantages over NR for homebrewing applications and shares all of its risks. The chemical compatibility ratings and the toxicology concern are identical.

For the full analysis of the N-nitrosamine precursor risk and the identification problem, see [Natural rubber (NR)](./12-nr.md). For the toxicology ranking, see [Toxicology and migration](../06-toxicology.md).
