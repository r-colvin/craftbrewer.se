# Nylon 12 (Polyamide 12)

| | ABNS | DES | Cleaning | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | A | A | A | A |

Nylon 12 appears in some equipment accessories and sealing components. It is a polyamide with a long, flexible aliphatic backbone (12 carbon atoms between amide linkages) that gives it low moisture absorption compared to shorter-chain nylons such as Nylon 6 and Nylon 6,6, making it better suited to aqueous environments.

## Food contact status

[EU Regulation 10/2011](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02011R0010-20230923) compliant. The caprolactam migration concern that applies to Nylon 6 (a known skin sensitiser with a strict SML of 15 mg/kg) does not apply to Nylon 12, which does not use caprolactam as a monomer.

## Compatibility — ABNS: A

The amide linkages in Nylon 12 are resistant to hydrolysis at the pH of working-dilution ABNS (pH 3.0–3.5). Acid hydrolysis of polyamides requires stronger acid concentrations and elevated temperatures than those encountered in homebrewing. A standard sanitise-and-drain cycle at ambient temperature is not a concern.

## Compatibility — DES / Cleaning / beer/wort: A

No concern across all three environments.
