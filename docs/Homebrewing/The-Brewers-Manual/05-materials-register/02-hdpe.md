# High-density polyethylene (HDPE)

| | ABNS | DES | PBW | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | *A* | *A* | *A* | *A* |

HDPE is used for fermenter taps (KegLand's preferred tap material), spray bottle bodies, storage containers, and chemical storage applications. It is a polyolefin like PP, but with a linear rather than branched backbone, giving it higher density, better chemical barrier properties, and better resistance to stress cracking than low-density variants.

## Food contact status

*A*-rated globally. [EU Regulation 10/2011](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02011R0010-20230923) compliant. HDPE is used for milk jugs, detergent bottles, and chemical storage containers — its food and chemical contact safety profile is extensively documented.

## Compatibility — ABNS: *A*

HDPE rates *A* for phosphoric acid and anionic surfactants at working dilution. The higher crystallinity of HDPE versus PP provides slightly better resistance to hydrocarbon and surfactant penetration, making it the marginally preferable choice for tap bodies where Zone B geometry concentrates WDC residue in thread roots. The [WDC model](../04-wdc-model.md) applies to HDPE as it does to all materials receiving ABNS contact that is allowed to dry — but at Zone A (fermenter body, tap bore), HDPE's rating under concentrated residue conditions remains *A*, and rinsing or cleaning between batches resets accumulation.

:::note WDC accumulation charts
Per-material accumulation charts — mapping WDC exposure against damage thresholds by zone — are a planned addition to this register.
:::

## Compatibility — DES: *A*

*A* at brief-to-moderate contact. A note for specific storage applications: at 80% ethanol in sustained contact over weeks to months, HDPE can show slow ethanol permeation and minor swelling. For a spray bottle that is refilled and emptied regularly on brew day, this is not a concern. For long-term DES storage, HDPE is acceptable but glass or specifically-rated alcohol storage containers are technically superior. For brew-day use, HDPE is *A*.

## Compatibility — PBW: *A*

No concern at homebrewing concentrations and temperatures.

## Compatibility — beer/wort: *A*

No concern.

---

*MDPE (medium-density polyethylene), sometimes found in older equipment, has similar compatibility to HDPE in all relevant brewing chemical environments.*
