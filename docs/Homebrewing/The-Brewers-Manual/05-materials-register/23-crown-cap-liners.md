# Crown cap liners (oxygen-scavenging)

| | ABNS | DES | PBW | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | *X* | *X* | — | *A* |

*X for ABNS and DES reflects functional deactivation, not structural damage to the cap itself. The cap seals correctly after contact; the oxygen-scavenging capacity does not.*

Oxygen-scavenging crown cap liners — such as Amorim Oxyguard and equivalent products — contain an iron-based chemistry that reacts irreversibly with oxygen. The mechanism: moisture activates the iron compound, which then scavenges oxygen from the headspace gas after the cap is applied. The liner's oxygen-scavenging capacity is finite and consumed over time.

## The sanitiser problem

If the liner is wetted with water or sanitiser before the cap is applied, the oxygen-scavenging chemistry is partially or fully consumed before it has done any useful work. The cap may seal mechanically — there is no structural failure — but the oxygen-scavenging function is depleted or gone.

This matters in practice because brewers sometimes sanitise all caps for a bottling session at the start, then apply them over the course of an hour or more. By the time later caps are applied, the scavenging chemistry has already reacted with atmospheric oxygen from the moment of wetting. The liner looks normal; the function is gone.

**Correct procedure:** Do not pre-sanitise oxygen-scavenging crown caps with any liquid sanitiser. Sanitise the bottles; apply caps directly from dry packaging without wetting the liner. The bottle interior has been sanitised; the cap needs only to be clean — manufacturer cleanliness from factory packaging is sufficient. Caps should be stored in their original sealed packaging until use and handled to avoid touching the liner surface.

This is the one case in this guide where the standard no-rinse sanitising process is actively contraindicated — not because of material harm, but because of functional deactivation.

## Food contact status

Crown cap liners are [EU Regulation 1935/2004](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02004R1935-20090807) compliant food contact materials. The oxygen-scavenging chemistry is approved for food contact in the quantities that migrate into beer headspace.

## Compatibility — ABNS: *X*

Functional deactivation. Do not apply.

## Compatibility — DES: *X*

Functional deactivation. Do not apply.

## Compatibility — beer/wort: *A*

The liner is designed for sustained contact with beer headspace. No concern.
