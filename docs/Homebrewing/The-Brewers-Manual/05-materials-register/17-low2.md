# LOW2

| | ABNS | DES | PBW | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | *B* | *A* | *A* | *A* |

LOW2 is KegLand's proprietary elastomer specification used for lid O-rings in the FermZilla All Rounder (post-2023, brown O-ring), the 5 L and 9 L mini kegs, and the Cornelius keg lid O-ring. The yellow-brown colour and ASTM D1418 M-class designation ("Synthetic M-Class and Cross-Linked Elastomers" per KegLand's product description) are most consistent with a specialised, high-crosslink-density EPDM formulation, though KegLand has not published the base polymer specification.

**Pre-2023 FermZilla All Rounder units use a black silicone O-ring, not LOW2.** Check the O-ring colour before assuming which specification applies.

## The oxygen transmission claim

The defining performance characteristic of LOW2 is oxygen transmission: KegLand states LOW2 achieves 27 times lower oxygen transfer than standard silicone or nitrile.[^1] This was independently tested by GunnLab, an Australian packaging testing laboratory, and the test report is available on the product page. A 27× reduction in oxygen permeation through the lid O-ring translates directly to extended shelf life for oxygen-sensitive beers stored in pressure vessels.

## Food contact status

EU food contact compliance for LOW2 specifically is an open item — pending written confirmation from KegLand EU. As an M-class elastomer in food contact use, it should be covered by applicable national food contact standards (BfR Recommendation or equivalent), but formal documentation has not been confirmed.

## Compatibility — ABNS: *B*

If the base polymer is EPDM-derived M-class, the same bounded analysis applies as for standard EPDM: adequate for brief wet contact during CO₂-purge sanitisation; the lid O-ring's groove geometry limits sustained immersion; one WDC cycle per sanitisation event, reset by post-batch cleaning. Rating: *B* — following EPDM chemistry, with the same bounded-contact caveat.

## Compatibility — DES / PBW / beer/wort: *A*

No concern across all three environments, following EPDM chemistry.
