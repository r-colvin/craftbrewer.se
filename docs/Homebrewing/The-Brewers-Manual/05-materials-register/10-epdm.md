# EPDM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer rubber)

| | ABNS | DES | PBW | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | *U/B* | *A* | *A* | *A* |

*U/B: U (unsatisfactory) under sustained immersion or repeated WDC without cleaning reset; B under realistic bounded contact with post-batch cleaning. See below.*

EPDM is the assumed material for the KegLand KL01625 fermenter airlock grommet — the standard black grommet supplied with Witre bucket fermenter kits. Confirmation from KegLand EU is an open item, but the black colour and hardness are consistent with EPDM rather than NR. EPDM also appears in fermenter tap washers and some sealing applications.

EPDM is a saturated-backbone elastomer (M-class per ASTM D1418), which provides significantly better oxidative and thermal stability than unsaturated-backbone rubbers like NR and SBR. It is the default food contact elastomer for many applications and is *A*-rated for most aqueous environments, including dilute acids and alkaline cleaners.

## Food contact status

EPDM is widely used in food contact applications and is covered by national food contact standards in EU member states. No harmonised EU regulation equivalent to Regulation 10/2011 exists for elastomers, but EPDM articles produced for food contact typically comply with relevant BfR recommendations (Germany) or equivalent national standards. Food contact grade specification should be confirmed for each component.

## Compatibility — ABNS: *U/B*

The phosphoric acid component of ABNS at working dilution rates *A* for EPDM — the saturated backbone has no susceptibility to acid hydrolysis across any brewing-relevant concentration range.

The DDBSA component tells a different story. The Rubber Group chemical compatibility chart rates EPDM as *U* (unsatisfactory) against benzenesulfonic acid — the closest available proxy for DDBSA.[^1] The mechanism: DDBSA's hydrophobic C12 alkyl chain partitions into the non-polar EPDM hydrocarbon matrix, causing swelling, plasticiser extraction, surface energy reduction, and progressive embrittlement.

The *U* rating reflects sustained immersion at high concentration. At working dilution (~300 ppm) and brief contact — the conditions of a normal fermenter sanitisation cycle — the practical risk is much lower. The critical question is what the [WDC model](../04-wdc-model.md) does to the grommet specifically.

For a fermenter airlock grommet, the WDC exposure is bounded: the grommet is wetted during the sanitisation event, fermentation humidity arrests the drying process for the duration of active fermentation, and post-batch cleaning resets accumulation. The grommet sees at most one meaningful partial WDC per brew — not an accumulating exposure. With normal brewing practice — PBW cleaning post-batch, ABNS at correct dilution — EPDM grommets are manageable. The rating for this realistic scenario is *B*.

The distinction between *U* (laboratory rating under sustained immersion) and *B* (realistic homebrewing scenario with post-batch cleaning) is not hand-waving — it is the point of the WDC model applied to a specific geometry.

## Compatibility — DES: *A*

EPDM is resistant to ethanol across the full concentration range.

## Compatibility — PBW: *A*

No concern at homebrewing concentrations and temperatures.

## Compatibility — beer/wort: *A*

No concern.

## EPDM versus the alternatives

EPDM is significantly preferable to NR or SBR for all homebrewing applications. If a grommet or tap washer is NR or SBR, it should be replaced with EPDM as a minimum. For the rationale, see [Natural rubber (NR)](./12-nr.md).

Silicone (platinum-cured) has a slightly better DDBSA resistance profile than EPDM (rated *B* versus *U* in the laboratory reference) and is the preferred material where correctly sized food-grade silicone components can be sourced. See [Silicone](./11-silicone.md).
