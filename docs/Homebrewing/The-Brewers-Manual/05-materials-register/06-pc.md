# Polycarbonate (PC)

| | ABNS | DES | PBW | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | *A* | *A* | *A* | *A*† |

*† Rating reflects chemical compatibility only. The toxicology concern — BPA migration — overrides the compatibility rating. See below.*

PC was used in older fermenter vessels and carboys. It has been largely eliminated from current homebrewing equipment due to BPA (bisphenol A) migration concerns. BPA is a recognised endocrine disruptor with an EU-harmonised specific migration limit of 0.05 mg/kg under [Regulation 10/2011](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02011R0010-20230923).

## Food contact status

PC is [EU Regulation 10/2011](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02011R0010-20230923) compliant with a specific migration limit for BPA of 0.05 mg/kg. Compliance requires that actual BPA migration from the article remains below this limit under tested conditions. However, BPA migration from PC accelerates at elevated temperatures and under alkaline cleaning conditions — PBW contact at temperatures above 40 °C is a particular concern. Accumulated cleaning cycles can cause PC articles to exceed BPA migration limits over time.

## Compatibility — ABNS / DES / PBW: *A* (chemical compatibility only)

PC is not dissolved or structurally degraded by ABNS, DES, or PBW at homebrewing concentrations. The chemical compatibility rating is *A*.

The toxicology concern is separate: PBW alkalinity accelerates BPA migration from PC, meaning chemically compatible cleaning progressively increases the BPA exposure risk from the article. The material is chemically stable; the migration product is the concern.

## Compatibility — beer/wort: *A*† (if undegraded)

No compatibility concern for beer contact. The BPA migration concern is the operative issue.

## Practical position

If you have PC equipment, replace it. The food contact standard has moved on — current fermenter options in PET, PP, and Tritan are better in every material respect, are BPA-free by design, and have better-documented food contact compliance. There is no scenario in which retaining PC equipment is preferable to replacing it with a current alternative.

For the toxicology context, see [Toxicology and migration](../06-toxicology.md).
