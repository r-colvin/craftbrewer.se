# Nitrile rubber (NBR)

| | ABNS | DES | PBW | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | *C* | *C* | *A* | *A* |

NBR is a copolymer of butadiene and acrylonitrile. It is not common in fermenter components but appears in some bottling wand seals, described as "food grade" in KegLand product pages. Its acrylonitrile (ACN) content — typically 18–50% by composition — determines its chemical resistance profile: higher ACN gives better oil and chemical resistance but reduced cold flexibility.

## Food contact status

Food contact grade NBR exists and is used in appropriate applications. The food contact grade specification should be confirmed for any NBR component in beer or wort contact — "food grade" labelling without specification details is a documentation gap.

## Compatibility — ABNS: *C*

NBR rates *B* for dilute phosphoric acid (the polar nitrile groups provide better acid resistance than NR) but *C* for DDBSA at working dilution — moderate swelling through hydrophobic partitioning of the C12 alkyl chain into the butadiene portion of the matrix. At CF=250 (WDC endpoint), the rating approaches *D*.

Brief contact during bottling wand sanitisation — a single DES or ABNS pass before use — is manageable. Sustained soaking of NBR seals in ABNS is not recommended.

## Compatibility — DES: *C*

NBR has limited ethanol resistance at 70–80% concentration. Swelling occurs, though less severely than NR. For a quick flush-and-drain sanitisation of a bottling wand, *C* is workable; for prolonged immersion, it is not.

## Compatibility — PBW: *A*

No concern.

## Compatibility — beer/wort: *A*

No concern for short-contact filling operations.
