# PTFE (Polytetrafluoroethylene)

| | ABNS | DES | PBW | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | *A* | *A* | *A* | *A* |

PTFE is chemically inert to essentially all reagents at homebrewing-relevant temperatures and concentrations. It is the most chemically inert polymer in common use and serves as the reference material for chemical resistance comparisons — when a compatibility chart says "compatible," that rating is often benchmarked against PTFE's behaviour.

PTFE appears in homebrewing as thread seal tape (plumber's tape), filter discs, and some gaskets and sealing surfaces.

## Food contact status

[EU Regulation 1935/2004](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02004R1935-20090807) compliant. PTFE is used extensively in food processing equipment, pharmaceutical manufacturing, and medical devices — its food contact and biocompatibility profile is among the most thoroughly documented of any polymer.

A separate regulatory consideration: the broader PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances) framework is evolving in the EU, with increasing regulatory scrutiny of fluorinated compounds as a class. PTFE itself — a high-molecular-weight, non-reactive polymer — is not currently subject to the same restrictions as short-chain PFAS compounds, but the regulatory environment around fluorinated materials is worth monitoring.

## Compatibility — ABNS / DES / PBW / beer/wort: *A*

No compatibility concern in any homebrewing chemical environment. No caveats.
