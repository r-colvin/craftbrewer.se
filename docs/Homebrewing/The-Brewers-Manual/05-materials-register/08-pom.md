# Polyoxymethylene (POM / Acetal)

| | ABNS | DES | PBW | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | *D–X* | *A* | *A* | *A*† |

*† Rating applies if undegraded. Degraded POM presents a formaldehyde migration risk — see below.*

POM — also known as acetal, Delrin, or polyformaldehyde — is the original material used in DuoTight push-fit fitting collars and remains the material in John Guest push-fit fittings. It is a high-precision engineering thermoplastic with excellent mechanical properties but a specific and serious vulnerability to acid sanitisers under wet-dry cycle conditions.

## The failure mechanism

The backbone of POM consists of ether-linked oxymethylene (–CH₂–O–) repeat units. Phosphoric acid — present in all ABNS sanitisers — acts as a catalyst for acid-catalysed chain-unzipping at these ether linkages. The backbone depolymerises progressively from the chain ends, releasing formaldehyde as it does so.

At working dilution and brief contact, the rate of this reaction is low. After WDC evaporation to CF=250, the phosphoric acid concentration in the dried residue approaches approximately 2.4 M — sufficient to drive chain-unzipping as a real and progressive process with each exposure event. The products of POM degradation include formaldehyde, which is a confirmed carcinogen (IARC Group 1) and has an EU specific migration limit of 15 mg/kg in food contact materials under [Regulation 10/2011](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02011R0010-20230923).[^1]

The DuoTight design revision document describes the failure mode explicitly: ABNS dried on POM collars under mechanical stress (Zone C geometry — the collar is permanently compressed by the push-fit mechanism) caused environmental stress cracking, leading to sudden structural failure of the fitting. This is the most complete cause-and-effect materials failure chain documented in the homebrewing literature.[^2]

## Food contact status

POM is [EU Regulation 10/2011](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02011R0010-20230923) listed with specific migration limits for formaldehyde (SML 15 mg/kg). Compliance requires that formaldehyde migration from undegraded POM articles remains within this limit. An article undergoing acid-catalysed depolymerisation — the WDC failure mode — is not in a compliant state.

## Compatibility — ABNS: *D–X*

*D* to *X* for repeated contact with drying. Brief contact without drying carries lower risk, but there is no scenario in normal sanitising practice where drying can be guaranteed not to occur. The rating is *D–X* rather than simply *X* because the rate of damage accumulation depends on contact geometry, number of WDC cycles, and whether cleaning events interrupt accumulation. The outcome if nothing changes is *X*.

**Do not use POM components in any surface that contacts ABNS sanitiser.**

## Compatibility — DES / PBW / beer/wort: *A*

POM is not attacked by ethanol, alkaline cleaners, or beer at ambient temperature.

## Identifying POM in equipment

POM is typically natural (white/off-white) or black, dimensionally precise, and has a distinctive faint sweet-chemical odour when machined or cut. It carries no standard consumer recycling code. John Guest fittings are POM unless explicitly stated otherwise. If a push-fit fitting is not the current KegLand DuoTight product (post-revision, confirmed POK collar) or is not explicitly identified as POK, treat it as potentially POM until confirmed.

For the replacement material, see [POK](./07-pok.md). For the toxicology context of formaldehyde migration, see [Toxicology and migration](../06-toxicology.md).
