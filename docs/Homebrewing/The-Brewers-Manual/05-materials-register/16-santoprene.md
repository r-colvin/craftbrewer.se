# Santoprene (Thermoplastic Vulcanisate)

| | ABNS | DES | Cleaning | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | A–B | A | A | A |

Santoprene is an EPDM/PP thermoplastic vulcanisate — a rubber-toughened thermoplastic that combines the chemical resistance profile of EPDM with the processing advantages of thermoplastics (it can be injection-moulded and recycled, unlike conventionally vulcanised elastomers). It appears in homebrewing as the KegLand Oxebar Gen2 elbow (confirmed by KegLand) and the NukaTap valve shuttle.

## Food contact status

Santoprene TPV is used in food contact applications. The specific formulation should be confirmed as food contact grade for each application.

## Compatibility — ABNS: A–B

Its chemical resistance follows EPDM for aqueous environments: **A** for phosphoric acid; **B** (bounded) for DDBSA under realistic contact conditions with post-batch rinsing or cleaning. The same [bounded WDC analysis](../04-wdc-model.md#the-grommet-as-a-bounded-case) that applies to EPDM grommets — one partial wet-dry cycle per brew, arrested by fermentation humidity, reset by rinsing or cleaning — applies here. For components like the Oxebar elbow, where the WDC geometry is more open than a compressed grommet, the effective exposure is even lower.

## Compatibility — DES / Cleaning / beer/wort: A

No concern across all three environments.
