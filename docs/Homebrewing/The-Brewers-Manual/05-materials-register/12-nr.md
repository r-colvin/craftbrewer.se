# Natural rubber (NR)

| | ABNS | DES | PBW | Beer/wort |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rating** | *D* | *C–D* | — | *D* |

:::danger Replace immediately
Natural rubber grommets, washers, and O-rings should be replaced without delay. The toxicology concern — N-nitrosamine precursor migration — is invisible to inspection. Intact, undegraded NR components present the same risk as visibly degraded ones. There is no acceptable threshold for this material in food contact applications where alternatives are available.
:::

Natural rubber is the vulcanised latex of *Hevea brasiliensis*. It remains in circulation in homebrewing equipment because it is cheap, elastic, and widely available. It should not be used in any food contact or sanitiser contact application.

## The toxicology concern

NR has a polyisoprene backbone with alternating double bonds — a structure susceptible to oxidation, ozone attack, and acid-catalysed degradation. Its typical cure system includes thiuram and dithiocarbamate accelerators.

These accelerators are the operative concern. Thiuram and dithiocarbamate compounds can release N-nitrosamine precursors and their degradation products into contact media. N-nitrosamines are classified by the International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) as probable or confirmed human carcinogens — Group 2A/Group 1 depending on the specific compound, with NDMA and NDEA classified Group 1 (carcinogenic to humans).[^1]

The critical point: this is a chemical migration risk, not a physical degradation risk. An NR grommet that is intact, correctly installed, and shows no visible degradation still releases the same precursor compounds. Visual inspection cannot provide reassurance.

## Food contact status

NR is [EU Regulation 1935/2004](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02004R1935-20090807) listed as a food contact material, but food contact compliance requires that N-nitrosamine and N-nitrosatable substance migration meet strict limits. The cure system accelerators are the source of this migration. Food contact grade NR exists — but budget grommets and washers are not typically produced to food contact grade specifications.

## Compatibility — ABNS: *D*

NR rates *D* to *U* for benzenesulfonic acid at working dilution — swelling, softening, and accelerated degradation of the unsaturated backbone. At WDC-concentrated levels (CF=250), the rating approaches *X*. The chemical incompatibility compounds the toxicology concern: ABNS contact both degrades NR and accelerates migration.

## Compatibility — DES: *C–D*

NR has poor ethanol resistance at 70–80% concentration. Swelling and extraction of vulcanisation accelerators occur.

## Compatibility — beer/wort: *D*

N-nitrosamine precursor migration into beer contact media is the primary concern. Sour beer contact at low pH with long conditioning times increases the migration rate.

## Practical position

Replace any NR grommets, washers, and O-rings immediately with EPDM as a minimum, or silicone where correctly sized components can be sourced. This is the single highest-priority action in the entire materials register — not because the absolute risk magnitude is the largest, but because the risk is invisible to inspection and entirely preventable.

NR components are often found in budget fermenter kits and older equipment. If you cannot confirm the grommet material, replace it.

For the broader toxicology ranking, see [Toxicology and migration](../06-toxicology.md).
