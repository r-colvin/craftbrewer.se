### Acid-based no-rinse sanitisers (ABNS)
The class term covering Star San, Sanipro Rinse, StellarSan, Chemsan, and ChemiPro San (with caveat). Active ingredient in all: DDBSA (dodecylbenzenesulphonic acid). Phosphoric acid as co-acidulant. Mechanism: acid pH disrupts cell membranes. Working concentration ~300 ppm DDBSA. EU biocide registration status. "No-rinse" — what it means and what it doesn't. Foam: not a problem, not an indicator of contamination.

### ChemiPro DES (ethanol-based)
Active ingredient: ethanol ~70–80%. Mechanism: protein denaturation. No non-volatile residue — WDC risk is zero (ethanol evaporates completely). Material compatibility: A-rated across all common homebrew materials. Why DES is the correct choice for BrewClub Level 1 and for specific use cases at all levels.

### ChemiPro San — the caveat
Same manufacturer contradicts itself on rinse/no-rinse status. Swedish and English instructions differ. No regulatory approval for no-rinse use confirmed. Position: treat as rinse-required until manufacturer provides formal EU-compliant no-rinse approval.

### The wet-dry cycle and ABNS
The wet-dry cycle model — explained in detail in [The wet-dry cycle model](./04-wdc-model.md) — describes what happens to ABNS residue left to dry on equipment. CF=250 concentrations, Zone A/B/C implications, and why post-brew cleaning is the primary safeguard.

### Spray bottle selection
Why bottle material matters for ABNS. Birchmeier McProper Plus P (manufacturer-confirmed ABNS compatible). EPDM-sealed bottles for DES. Viton bottles: peroxide-cured only — polyamine-cured rated "limited" for DDBSA.

### Long-term storage in equipment
StellarSan in FermZilla: confirmed safe by KegLand — leave-in indefinitely. ABNS in Corny keg: stainless, safe. General principle: ABNS storage is appropriate for smooth-bore vessels with compatible seals.

### Heat and ethanol as sanitisers
When chemicals aren't needed: boiling wort self-sanitises the kettle; silicone hose sanitised by boiling or flow-through ABNS; thermometer probe, hydrometer, and test jar by DES flush or boiling water.
