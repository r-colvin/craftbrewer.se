# No-chill brewing

No-chill brewing is a technique where wort is transferred directly from the kettle into a sealed, heat-rated vessel immediately after the boil, without first cooling it to pitching temperature. The vessel is sealed, allowed to cool overnight or over 24–48 hours at ambient temperature, and yeast is pitched once the wort reaches the target fermentation temperature.

The technique is common in Australia and New Zealand, where water costs and ambient temperatures make rapid chilling impractical, and has been adopted by homebrewers worldwide as a way to simplify the brew day and eliminate the need for a wort chiller.
