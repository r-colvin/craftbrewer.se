# FermZilla All Rounder (30 L)

:::note EU focus
This page covers the KegLand FermZilla All Rounder available through KegLand EU. Component specifications are based on KegLand documentation; several material confirmations are pending from KegLand EU (noted throughout). A 20 L FermZilla does not exist — the 20 L fermentation option in the BrewClub context is the Witre PP bucket (see [bucket fermenters](./01-bucket-fermenter.md)).
:::

The FermZilla All Rounder is a 30 L pressure-capable PET fermenter designed for pressure fermentation, closed transfer, and extended-storage conditioning. It is the step up from the bucket fermenter for brewers who want pressure fermentation capability, clearer beer through pressure conditioning, or a larger batch size. Its material profile is well-suited to all standard brewing chemicals; the key constraints are temperature (the PET vessel) and a small number of component material confirmations that remain pending from the manufacturer.

## Vessel — PET

The vessel body and lid are clear PET, pressure-rated to 2.5 bar (36.2 psi). All PET material analysis from the [materials register](../05-materials-register/03-pet.md) applies directly.

*A*-rated for ABNS at working dilution (all durations, ambient temperature), DES, and alkaline cleaners within the temperature constraint described below. Food contact appropriate.

**What PET is not:** KegLand states that PET is "impermeable" compared to HDPE — this is directionally correct (PET has lower gas and aroma permeability than HDPE) but imprecise. PET is a partial oxygen barrier, not an impermeable one. Plain PET OTR is approximately 0.04–0.08 mL O₂ per day per 500 mL; for a 30 L vessel with proportionally more surface area, total wall oxygen ingress is meaningful for extended conditioning. The FermZilla is appropriate for fermentation (where yeast consumes oxygen actively) and for conditioning up to several weeks. For indefinite storage of finished, oxygen-sensitive beer, stainless is the more conservative choice.

## Lid ring — GR engineering plastic, inferred POK

KegLand describes the lid ring as "glass-reinforced engineering plastics — not only chemical resistant, heat resistant but also tough." This description is consistent with GR-POK (glass-reinforced polyketone) — the material KegLand uses across their engineering plastic components, including DuoTight collars. POK is *A*-rated for all brewing chemicals. Formal confirmation from KegLand EU has been requested and is pending.

## Lid O-ring — pending confirmation

The lid O-ring is the most analytically significant unknown in this equipment. It is the primary pressure seal; it contacts wort and beer during fermentation; and it contacts ABNS during cleaning and storage. Its material compatibility with ABNS is directly relevant.

KegLand states that all units manufactured after 01/01/2023 have the current brown O-ring as standard, described as "newer material that is softer and easier to fit."[^1] The brown colour and "softer" description are consistent with LOW2 or EPDM compounding. It is not described as silicone, and the description does not match the yellow LOW2 material used in Cornelius keg lids.

**Working assumption:** treat as LOW2 or EPDM until confirmed. Both are manageable for normal brewing use. Neither warrants any process change before confirmation. If the O-ring material is confirmed as NR (which would be inconsistent with a pressure fermentation vessel specification but cannot be excluded without confirmation), immediate replacement would be required.

Inspect the lid O-ring at every brew. It should be supple, seated evenly in its groove, and free of cracking or permanent deformation. Replace annually regardless of apparent condition — it is a consumable under compressive load.

## StellarSan storage — confirmed safe

StellarSan (working-dilution ABNS) can be stored in the FermZilla indefinitely between batches. This is confirmed by KegLand documentation.[^2] The PET vessel, POK carbonation caps, and silicone dip tube are all appropriate for sustained ABNS contact. The lid O-ring — pending material confirmation — is the only component where sustained ABNS storage carries a theoretical question; in practice, the use is widely reported without issue.

The US retailer guidance that stated "one hour maximum" was incorrect and has been superseded. KegLand's own documentation is the authoritative source.

## BlowTie spunding valve

The BlowTie is a diaphragm-type spunding valve with integrated pressure gauge. The body is POK — confirmed, *A*-rated. The diaphragm material is unconfirmed (pending KegLand EU). In normal use the BlowTie contacts CO₂ gas only; beer contact occurs only in the event of suck-back during an over-pressure event. The gas-contact-only path reduces the practical significance of the diaphragm material for most uses.

## Dip tube — silicone

Silicone is *B*-rated for ABNS. The dip tube contacts beer throughout fermentation and carries the full ABNS flow during sanitisation. Silicone absorbs DDBSA over time, which progressively extracts aromatic compounds from beer — a flavour concern (absorbed hops, esters) rather than a safety one. The dip tube is a consumable; inspect for discolouration and replace if aroma carry-over between batches is detectable.

## Cleaning

**Temperature constraint — PET:** maximum 40°C for cleaning solutions, maximum 30 minutes soak time. PBW at 40°C for 30 minutes is the upper limit; StellarClean at cold-to-warm temperature with extended soak is the more practical choice for PET. See [cleaning](../03-cleaning.md) for the rationale.

**Cleaning protocol:**
1. Immediate rinse post-fermentation — add 2–3 L cold water, swirl, drain via carbonation cap. Do this before the equipment cools.
2. StellarClean at warm temperature (≤40°C), fill to ~5 L, soak 15–20 minutes, shake gently. No abrasive cleaning inside the PET vessel.
3. Drain through the liquid carbonation cap. Rinse thoroughly with water until pH-neutral.
4. Inspect the lid O-ring while disassembled.
5. Sanitise and store with ABNS fill, or dry-store clean with lid loosely fitted.

Do not use hot PBW on PET. Do not use abrasive cloths or brushes inside the vessel. The 120 mm lid opening allows a soft cloth or long-handled soft brush; the wide opening is a genuine design advantage over narrow-neck fermenters.

## Pressure vessel service life

The FermZilla is rated to 2.5 bar. Pressure capability is a function of the vessel integrity and the lid seal. Service life depends on:

- **Lid O-ring condition:** inspect and replace annually, or on any sign of deformation, cracking, or visible distress. A failed lid O-ring during pressure fermentation means loss of pressure and potentially a carbonation loss event, not a safety hazard at homebrewing pressures — but it is an avoidable failure.
- **Vessel inspection:** check for scratches, crazing, or discolouration of the PET at each cleaning. A scratched interior should prompt replacement — scratches increase microplastic release and create biofilm harbourage.
- **DuoTight/POK carbonation caps:** inspect the push-fit collar for chalking or surface change. POK is very resistant to ABNS; the concern is mechanical wear at the push-fit rather than chemical attack.
