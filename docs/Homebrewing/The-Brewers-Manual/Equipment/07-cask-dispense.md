# Cask dispense

:::note Status
**Stub** — content to be written.
:::

**Covers:** Cask ale dispense for homebrewers — spiles, shives, keystones, taps, and the sustained wet storage considerations unique to cask.

**Components:**
- Shive: the bung that fits the large hole in the cask head
- Keystone: the smaller fitting that accepts the tap
- Spile: the peg that goes through the shive to control venting — hard spile (seals gas), soft spile (allows venting)
- Tap: Pint365 or equivalent — material specification unconfirmed (open item: contact Pint365)
- Cask itself: typically stainless (pin or firkin)

**Key distinction from keg:** Cask is not pressurised with exogenous CO₂. Residual fermentation provides the carbonation. Dispense is by gravity or hand pump (beer engine). Once tapped, the cask is open to atmosphere — spoilage risk increases with time.

**Sustained wet storage analysis:**
- Unlike keg, cask equipment is in sustained contact with beer for the duration of dispense
- WDC analysis does not apply — no dry cycle occurs during service
- Relevant concern is sustained leaching from elastomer components, not WDC concentration
- Spile material: wood (soft spile) or MDPE (hard spile) — both acceptable
- Tap seal material: unconfirmed — priority open item
