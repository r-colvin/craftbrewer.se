# Cornelius kegs and mini kegs (stainless)

:::note Status
**Stub** — content to be written.
:::

**Covers:** Stainless steel Cornelius kegs and stainless mini kegs — cleaning advantages, O-ring materials, pitting prevention, and passivation.

**Vessel material:** Stainless steel 304 or 316L — inert to all homebrewing chemicals at working concentrations. The stainless vessel itself requires no compatibility assessment. See [Stainless steel](../05-materials-register/19-stainless.md).

**O-rings (the critical component):**
- LOW2 (KegLand): brown O-rings, post-2023 standard. EU food contact status pending confirmation. See [LOW2](../05-materials-register/17-low2.md).
- Silicone: acceptable alternative. See [Silicone](../05-materials-register/11-silicone.md).
- NR (black O-rings in some older or budget keg sets): replace immediately — same N-nitrosamine precursor risk as NR grommets. See [NR](../05-materials-register/12-nr.md).

**Cleaning advantages over PET:**
- Stainless tolerates high temperatures, caustic cleaners, and extended soak times without degradation
- Smooth interior surface — no micro-scratches to harbour biofilm
- Dishwasher-safe (lid and posts removed)

**Pitting prevention:**
- Use low-chloride water when mixing PBW or ABNS solutions
- Do not use chlorine-based sanitisers (bleach) without subsequent acid rinse
- Chloride ions cause pitting corrosion on stainless — slow but cumulative

**Passivation:**
- Citric acid passivation recommended after any significant chemical or mechanical abrasion to the interior surface
- Restores the passive oxide layer on stainless

**Serving system integration:**
- Ball lock posts and disconnects: POK (KegLand DuoTight), stainless, or brass
- EVABarrier line: food contact compliant, low O₂ transmission. See [EVABarrier](../05-materials-register/18-evabarrier.md).
- NukaTap: stainless faceplate, POK internals
