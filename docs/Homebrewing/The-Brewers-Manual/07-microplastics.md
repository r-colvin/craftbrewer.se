# Microplastics

:::note Status
**Stub** — content to be written.
:::

**Covers:** Microplastics in homebrewing — what the evidence says, what it doesn't, and a calibrated precautionary position.

**Key points:**
- Sources of microplastics are predominantly environmental, not equipment-derived
- ANSES 2025 data: glass bottles measured higher microplastic counts than PET bottles in some measurements — context and mechanism explained (environmental contamination during manufacture and filling, not material migration)
- Chemical leaching from plastics is a more tractable and better-characterised concern than microplastic particulates
- Physical damage (scratching, abrasion) increases particulate release — connects to "no abrasive cleaning" and "replace visibly damaged equipment" recommendations
- Precautionary position: minimise unnecessary plastic contact time with finished beer; choose equipment with [EU Regulation 10/2011](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:02011R0010-20230923) food contact compliance documentation

**Regulatory context:** EU Regulation 10/2011 migration limits; EFSA microplastics assessment.
