### Cleaning vs. sanitising
Not the same thing. Cleaning removes organic soil. Sanitising reduces microbial load on a clean surface. Sanitising over organic contamination is ineffective. Sequence is non-negotiable: clean first, sanitise second.

### The Sinner Circle
Four factors that together determine cleaning effectiveness: chemistry, temperature, mechanical action, time. Reducing one requires increasing others. Relevant to every cleaning decision.

### Alkaline cleaners — PBW and StellarClean
Mechanism: alkaline hydrolysis of proteins and fats. Working concentration, temperature requirements, contact time. PBW vs. StellarClean: same chemistry, different brands. When to use which.

### Oxidising cleaners — StellarOxy and equivalents
Mechanism: oxidation. Use cases: organic staining, biofilm, post-dry-hop residue. Compatibility constraints — not for all metals or elastomers.

### Post-use immediate rinse
The single highest-impact action in the entire cleaning sequence. Rinsing equipment immediately after use — before organic soil dries — reduces cleaning time and chemical requirement dramatically. This is the number one practical recommendation.

### Water alone

### Temperature constraints by material
